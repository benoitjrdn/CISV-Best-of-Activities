# Cookies Economics

### Goal

Mimic the economy. Think about ressources allocation. 

### Particular requirements

The activity requires a kitchen with an oven that the camp is allowed to use.
Else, try to substitute the cookies with another, raw recipe. 
You also need ingredients to bake cookies for $n$ participants (+ adult group !)

### Description

Groups of kids have (the same) instructions for a cookies recipe, and are given ingredients.
Some are missing a part of the ingredients (not enough flour), some are missing the whole ingredients (no chocolate at all).
There is enough for the whole camp, but the participants need to realize it first, and to trade effectively.

#### Alternative Versions

Maybe try to not make everyone bake cookies but each a different thing for older camps, in order not to nudge the idea too much.

### Instructions

If you can disguise this activity as a "fun" one, it kills the doubt for "solution seekers".
Split in groups.
Give the ingredients to the leader.
If groups of 8, make the recipe say "for eight people".

Any way they find to allocate ressources efficiently if fine. If they don't, it's fine. If they eat the chocolate raw, it's annoying, but it's fine.
If they bake with just dry ingredients because they are missing milk and eggs, well it's fine too. Learning by doing (mistakes).


> [!tip] 
> Staffies want to eat too, make a group of Leaders & JCs that aren't participating and make them cook for the adult group.
