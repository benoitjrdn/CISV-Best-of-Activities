# World Maps Projections and Us
source : Ben

### Goal

Learn about world maps, and how they are not neutral.

### Description

#### Part 1

> Draw a map

Ask the kids to draw a map of the world, from memory.
The kids will probably draw their country in the middle. Some countries will be bigger, some forgotten...

Do not intervene.
They should not copy each other (especially cross-delegation).

Alternatively, they can do it by delegation.

#### Part 2

Maps are a 2D projection of a 3D world. All 2D maps are wrong, in some way, but have their advantages.
When making a map, you will have to distort some things. 
Also, our planet is a huge rock flying in space, there is no real "up" or "down", this is why Australia-centered maps look different from Europe-centered maps.

Watch this video : [Why all world maps are wrong - YouTube](https://youtu.be/kIID5FDi2JQ)

#### Part 3

Suggest a more neutral map, like the one shown below.
*It's called the North Polar Azimuthal Equidistant Projection*
Print it out.

If you have a globe that's even better.

#### Part 4

Now the kids take a pin and place it where they live.
Now you have a world map of where all people are from.
