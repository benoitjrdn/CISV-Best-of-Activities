# Baranga


> [!todo] 
> - [ ] [CISV::todo-elaborate]

### Goal

Educate about cultural and societal differences

### Description

The participants are taught a cards game, that they must play without talking.
They don't know that they were taught different rules, which creates confusion, and frustration because they think the other one is cheating.

### Instructions

#### Set-up

- Divide participants into 5 subgroups, each at a separate table.
- Deal 4 cards to each player per game.
- Shared base rule for all tables:
  - The person who wins the trick plays the next card.
- Each table has its own secret set of rules.
- Participants are instructed not to reveal their rules to other groups.

#### Table Rules

> [!info]- Table 1 instructions
> 
> 1. Dealer can be anyone; person to the right of the dealer starts.
> 2. Ace is the highest card; no trump suit.
> 3. First player of each trick may play any suit. Others must follow suit.
> 4. If unable to follow suit, play any card. Trick is won by the highest card of the original suit.
> 5. Winner of the trick plays the next card.

> [!info]- Table 2 instructions
> 1. Dealer can be anyone; person to the right of the dealer starts.
> 2. Ace is the lowest card; diamonds are trump.
> 3. First player of each trick may play any suit. Others must follow suit.
> 4. If unable to follow suit, play trump. If no trump, play any card. Trick is won by the highest card of the original suit.
> 5. Winner of the trick plays the next card.

> [!info]- Table 3 instructions
> 6. Dealer can be anyone; person to the left of the dealer starts.
> 7. Ace is the lowest card; clubs are trump.
> 8. First player of each trick may play any suit. Others must follow suit.
> 9. If unable to follow suit, play trump. If no trump, play any card. Trick is won by the highest card of the original suit.
> 10. Winner of the trick plays the next card.

> [!info]- Table 4 instructions
> 11. Dealer can be anyone; the person with the lowest trump starts.
> 12. Ace is the highest card; clubs are trump.
> 13. First player of each trick may play any suit. Others must follow suit.
> 14. If unable to follow suit, play trump. If no trump, play any card. Trick is won by the highest card of the original suit.
> 15. Winner of the trick plays the next card.

> [!info]- Table 5 instructions
> 16. First round: dealer can be anyone. From the second round on, the dealer rotates clockwise. The dealer starts the game.
> 17. Ace is the lowest card; no trump suit.
> 18. First player of each trick may play any suit. Others must follow suit.
> 19. If unable to follow suit, play any card. Trick is won by the highest card of the original suit.
> 20. Winner of the trick plays the next card.

#### Round Instructions

- Each round lasts 5 minutes.
- Play as many tricks as possible during that time.
- After the first round, remove the rules from the table.
- No speaking allowed.
- Participants may only use gestures or drawings.
- No written or spoken communication.

##### Movement Between Tables (after each round)

- The player with the most tricks moves one table clockwise.
- The player with the fewest tricks moves one table counterclockwise.
- All others stay at the same table.
- Ties are resolved by rock-paper-scissors.

### Suggested Debrief

- How did it feel when communication was restricted?
- How did you react when others played differently than expected?
- What parallels can you draw with real-life cultural misunderstandings?
