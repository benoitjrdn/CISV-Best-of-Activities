 # Privilege Walk

### Goal

Realize one's own privileges & that not everyone has the same opportunity.
Should not be done too early on camp.

### Description

The participants are blindfolded and are getting different statements from the Organizer.
If they apply, they take a step forward.
At the end, they take their blindfolds off.
This is a (mostly) silent activity.

### Instructions

Get blindfolds for everyone.
Make the kids stand in a line.
Read the statements out loud, one by one.
Do a translation time for each difficult question. Take initiative on when to translate, if possible, to avoid revealing the kids position.
Leaders should try to avoid correcting their kids. This is a silent activity and might make people snap out of the game, in addition to potentialy embarassing the kid.
As a Leader, take notes if you disagree with the kid, and tell them either privately or during debrief.

#### Questions

### Suggested Debrief

- How did it feel at the end of the activity, when you saw where you ended up on the ladder ? 
- Did the result surprise you? Why?
- Were there any questions you thought were difficult to answer and why? 
- How did it feel to let go of the person next to you (or to stay linked together)?
- How do you think your privilege affects you in your daily life?
- Is privilege something you think about, or talk about with your friends and family?
- How do you think being privileged or underprivileged affects people’s lives?
- Have you ever felt that your social status has affected you having or not having possibilities in life? 
- Do you have friends, or do you know someone, that you consider more or less privileged than you? How does that make you feel?
- Do you think we tend to mix with people with the same social status as our own? Why?
- How do you think differences in privilege affects society locally? Globally?
- How can we better understand our privilege? 
- What can we do to make people more aware of their privilege and how this affects society?
