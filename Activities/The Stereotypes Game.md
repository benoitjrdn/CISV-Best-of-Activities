# The Stereotypes Game

> [!attention] 
> While commonly referred to as "The Stereotypes Game", try to find another name like "The Association Game".
> Stereotypes are often misunderstood as prejudice and are 
> While terminological debates are mostly useless, these specific words carry heavy connotations, and could shift the outcome of the activity, partially because it might implicitly influence the Leaders too. 

### Goal

Reflect on stereotypes, prejudices, judgement.

### Description

Similar to [[First and Last Impression]], but generalized to the country.
Can be done in two sessions, the second one being what you think of the country now.

#### Alternative Versions


### Instructions

Sit by delegations.
You get a big sheet of paper and write the name of your country on it.
You pass your sheet to the right.
On the one you just got, you write what the country makes you think of.
Switch every few minutes.
Eventually, you get your original paper back.
The Leader first reads it to check if everything is ok (no nazi symbols on the german one, it's a classic, the kids don't realize it can hurt).
Then as a group they see what people think of when they think of their country.
You can also, delegation by delegation, present what was written on it, say what is true, and what isn't.

Towards the end of camp, you can make a part 2, on the back of the original sheet, where they write what they learned about your country.
