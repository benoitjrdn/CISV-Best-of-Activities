# Fake News

> [!attention] 
> Debrief is suuuuper important !
> Else you could break the kids' trust.
> Do no neglect translations. Sit by delegations, and leaders ask their kids if they understood.
> Make sure that none of the fake news are targetting a single person, especially a minor.
> Be very mindful of stereotypes, insecurities, etc etc. Even if it involves the whole group, it can be shaming ("the boys bathroom is super dirty").

### Goal

Learn about fake news and how easily they spread, and how debunking doesn't spread as fast.

### Description

Have you heard about the fact that we eat 8 spiders a year during our sleep ? Well that's a lie. It was spread to see how fast fake news went around. When it was revealed that it was an experiment, this time it didn't spread, despite the effort. Because debunks are less juicy. And because we do not think as critically as we would like to think we do.

In this activity, the adult group spread fake news (**agreed on, and which you keep track of**).
The kids are not aware that something is going on.
The fake news can be very obviously fake or even turn out wrong during the day, **you don't hide the truth**, the point is that some or all of them can be debunked.
At the end of the day, organize a big explanation and debrief on the spread of fake news. 

### Instructions

#### Create fake news

- juicy - so they will spread
- easily debunkable
- keep track of every single one of them (important)

> [!list] 
> - [ ] `Delegation X` is flying back home 3 days early 
> - [ ] The kitchen burned, we're gonna be skipping lunch
> - [ ] `Leader A` and `Leader B` are a couple
> - [ ] `JC 1` is super sick and won't be able to leave bed until in at least three days
> - [ ] One of the boys room made the smoke alarm go off because they used too much deodorant
> - [ ] `Leader A` and `Leader B` are super upset at each other and don't talk anymore
> - [ ] The campsite is an old military base
> - [ ] There is a secret bunker underneath the campsite
> - [ ] Another delegation is coming for the last week of camp
> - [ ] Camp is ending a few days before
> - [ ] `Famous person` is retiring
> - [ ] `Famous person` is running for president

#### Spread the news... and debunks

- spread the news in front of the kids, don't directly adress them, talk to another Leader, JC, Staff
- for some of them, leak the debunk at one point (they don't spread as much)
- never lie 
	- if asked if something is true, don't say yes, say "so I've heard"
	- if someone asks you if what another Leader said is true, do not cover their lie, say you don't know
- Later in the day, you can start spreading some debunks too

### Suggested Debrief

> [!important] 
> Emphasize that it is the only time you lied during camp, for the sake of the activity, and there won't be a second one.
> Remove all doubts.

Keep track of all the news you have spread.
Reveal each single one of them.

- [ ] Did you hear any of those rumours ?
	- [ ] How did you react
	- [ ] Did you doubt any ?
		- [ ] Did you ask people for their source ?
	- [ ] Was there a rumour that was debunked, but you didn't even realize ? *(lunch & burnt kitchen)*
