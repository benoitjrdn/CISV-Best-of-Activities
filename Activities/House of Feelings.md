# House of Feelings

### Goal

Have fun.
While this activity has practically no content, it is often remembered after camp.

> [!comment] also see [[On Simulation Games]]
> This activity is often described as a simulation game, and one of the obsoletes one : it has no purpose, and is labeled "potentially dangerous" because you mess with the kids' sleep schedule.
> So people avoid it.
> Honestly, I think that if you make sure the kids have no trouble sleeping, sure it has no content but it's kinda fun.
> I also don't see how this fits into the group of simulation games, and I also think that simulation games have now (wrongfully) become negatively connoted. 
> Just discuss it with your group you guys are 21+.

### Description

You wake up the kids, one by one, blindfold them, and let them do a tour of sensory experience.

The next day, you act as if they dreamed of it.
You'll be exhausted.

#### Alternative Versions

> [!hint] 
> In the original version, you wake up the kids in the middle of the night, and act as if they had a dream. You don't need to do that. But then the activity makes no real sense.
