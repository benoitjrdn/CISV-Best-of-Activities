# Surviving Islands

### Goal

Ponder on cooperation.

### Description

The kids are on different "islands", outside.
They have to survive for a set number of rounds (say 8), by consuming a candy at the end of the round. They can steal some from the other groups under specific conditions.
If they don't eat their candy, they're out until the end of the game (not round, game).
The goal is to survive.

The twist is that there is much more candy than needed to survive. If the kids share their resources, everybody survives. But they need to realize it first.

### Instructions

#### Materials

- a lot of candies (use gummy bears for instance), you need about 20-30 times the amount of kids. Alternatively, cut paper pieces. Be mindful of the wind.
- a bowl for each island, in which to put the candies
- des plots pour délimiter les îles

#### Planning

The planning of the teams must be thought through.
Group some islands by personality : the thinkers, the impulsives, the altruists...
Some islands are placed very strategically, in a corner, easy to defend, others in the middle.
The sizes of the islands varies. The size of the groups varies. The amount of resources (candies) varies.
Make sure each kid is with someone who can understand them (they can be put by delegation if needed).
Each team has a leader that is in charge of the bowl of candy and that makes sure the kids play fairly.


### Suggested Debrief

> [!list] Debrief inspiration
> Debrief in a big group. 
> *Assuming they didn't cooperate :*
> - [ ] FT5 to check the correlation between winning an resources
> - [ ] How did you feel about this game ? *asking the kids who lost first*
> - [ ] Raise your hand if you realized there was an unfair distribution of resources
> 	- [ ] How did you react to that information ?
> - [ ] Raise your hand if you tried to make deals with other teams
> 	- [ ] How did these deals look like ?
> 	- [ ] *See who proposed, see who accepted, see who refused, ask for motives, see who broke promises...*
> - [ ] (How did it feel to be on the team with the most / least amount of resources ?)
> - [ ] (Did you consider that by cooperating, you could improve someone else's position without losing ?)
> - [ ] Are there any real life situations this makes you think of ?
