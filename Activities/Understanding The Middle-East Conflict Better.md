# Understanding The Middle-East Conflict Better
Judaism is not Zionism

> [!danger] 
> This is a really really sensitive topic.
> Do NOT perform this activity when CISV Israel is involved. Not only because of the Israeli children, but also because their government could potentially shut down their branch of CISV.  
> Check with every single Leader, Staff and JC if it is okay for them.

### Goal

Deescalate antijudaism.
Differentiate between the religion that is Judaism and Zionism.
Differentiate between Israel as a country, the government of Israel, Israelis, and jewish people from all around the world.
Explain how the actions of the current government of Israel are war crimes.

> [!info] Argumentation of the use of "antijudaism" instead of "antisemitism"
> 
> "Semites" describes folks of middle eastern regions.
> During WW2, the word "antisemitism" was used because it sounds scientific, and was a way to justify hatred against jewish people, not at all the rest of the middle east.
> Linguistically, this makes no sense, but it stuck.
> 
> A lot of people trying to justify Israel's actions will accuse people of antisemitism, performing the use of a strawman arguement and of a false dichotomy. 
> While it is true that some people hate Israel purely because of hatred towards jewish people, that doesn't mean that every attack on Israel has to come from said hatred.
> Israel is not immune to any wrongdoing because of history of course. The sole existence of antisemitism doesn't mean that every single decision the government of Israel takes is perfect and uncriticizable, and could only be criticized because you hate jewish people.
> 
> Because I want to more clarity, and I want to let go - to the extent possible - of this very very loaded word, oftentimes deriving the debate, I chose the word of antijudaism instead.


Learn about Israels war crimes, notably against Palestine, while keeping it as neutral as possible, all while 



Differentiate between Judaism and Zionism.
Differentiate between a government and its people.
