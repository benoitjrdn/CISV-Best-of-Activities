# Spectrum Thinking Activity

### Goal

Realize most things aren't binary.
Unlock new horizons in personal reflections.

### Description

Participants get asked a bunch of questions (where opinions are divided).

> [!hint]
>  You as the Leader have to be quite vague about the instructions, to not influence the participant's decision into binary / spectrum thinking.

Tell them to go to point `A` if you think `x`, and to `B` if you think `y`.
Start off with quite binary questions.
Then introduce questions that are more nuanced, and where people can have ambivalent thoughts on.
See if the kids initiate standing inbetween the two points to signify a nuanced opinion.
After some time, JCs / Leaders can jump in and go to 
Followed by a debrief that is mostly a discussion.

### Instructions

> [!list] Suggested Questions
> - [ ] I prefer pizza / I prefer french fries
> - [ ] I prefer winter / summer
> - [ ] It's better to be fast / precise
> - [ ] It's better to always tell the truth / It's better to protect people by lying to them
> - [ ] Rules are made to be followed / to be broken
> - [ ] You chose who you want to become / You are destined from the moment you are born
> - [ ] You can / can't separate the art from the artist
> - [ ] I am introverted / extroverted
> - [ ] It's better to live in the city / on the land 
> - [ ] Freedom / Security is  more important
> - [ ] Money makes / doesn't make happiness
> - [ ] You should always forgive / Someone who has done a wrong thing once will do it again

### Suggested Debrief

> [!question] Debrief
> - [ ] *fist to 5*
> - [ ] *feedback*
> - [ ] Where there any points in the activity where you were frustrated because you couldn't express your opinion very well ?
> 	- [ ] Was it before or after starting to stand on a continuum ?
> - [ ] Were you annoyed that you had to choose at some points ?
> - [ ] Do you know what a spectrum is ? 
> 	- [ ] *ask for what they think it means, then explain*
> - [ ] *Lecture time*
> 	- [ ] I hope it made you realize that, even if it's easier for us to see things as "either / or" (or in black and white / binary), most things in life are way more complicated.
