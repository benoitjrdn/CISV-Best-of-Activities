# Bored Jury

### Goal

> [!important] Camp life activity
> Perform only under certain circumstances

Make the participants understand what it feels like to plan activities for people who do not care.
This activity is meant to be used in instances where participants don't get involved in camp life and activities, not listening to the leaders and disturbing them.

### Description

This is a frustrating activity for the kids.
The kids plan an activity for the leaders, with apparently a reward for the best activity, but the leaders don't engage in it, to show what it feels like to have a group not paying attention

### Instructions

You make them plan activities in groups, for the leaders.
You tell them they have to prepare the rules, materials if needed, and make the leaders play it.
One group by one, while the others wait somehwere else, they go explain the activity, but the adults are not listening for the most part, some might be playing with a ball, laying on the ground, talking, going away... Some can listen too.
You then explain to them that this was the purpose of  the activity,

> [!important]
> Do not target a specific kid !
> Even if some kids are more annoying than others, and while in the role you might want to mimic them, with no bad intentions, if they think they are meant it can be really hurting !

If the kids really liked their activity, you can try to make an activity where they can actually plan something for the group.
Or keep it for [[Opposites Day]].

### Suggested Debrief

- [ ] How did it feel like not being listened to ?
